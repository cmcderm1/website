FAQ

My background in marketing and content creation has caused me to work with numerous clients who are building new websites. I quickly gained an affinity for front-end web development and decided to attain a certification in web development. I love being able to bring peoples' ideas to life, and web development is all about making it happen. Every new project can mean a host of new questions that I get to solve. There's nothing quite like it.
I like to keep my design process fairly simple. First, I try to get an understanding of the client that I am working with and their current aesthetic, whether it be their current branding elements, flyers, messaging, and/or market. Then I communicate with my client to understand their design goals and samples of designs that they like. Once I know the direction of my project, I get moving. Throughout the design process clear communication and client approval is a MUST.
Being a web developer means having the skillset to work with clients in very diverse fields. From consulting firms to nonprofit organizations, I typically work with clients who are looking for a developer who know what questions to ask and can offer other skills as well, like writing and marketing strategy.
Every project is unique, which means the turnaround time for two different projects can vary. I try to complete a website within 1-2 months tops. For simple designs, I can complete a website in 1-2 weeks. Much of my pace depends on the collaboration between myself and my client. The more information, clarification, and content that I am given at the beginning and throughout the development process, the quicker my turnaround.
